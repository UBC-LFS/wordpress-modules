[accordion title="Parking Stall"]

Parking Dept Vehicles

UBC parking has marked our parking spots for departmental vehicles as follows: Stalls R274, R275 and R276 (the Dodge Caravan, Ford Ranger and Prius can be parked in any of these stalls). Stall R273 is reserved for the Dean's use only. As usual, all other stalls in the area have been assigned for other purposes, and our vehicles will be towed if parked in any other spaces.

What if an unauthorized user is parked in the LFS departmental spots?

  1. Record the license plate (preferably take a photo), make and model of the unauthorized vehicle as well as the specific parking spot (i.e. R273, R274, R275 or R276).  Email this information to lewis.fausak@ubc.ca and cc andy.jeffries@ubc.ca along with the vehicle that you are driving (i.e. Prius, Caravan, or Ranger).
  2. If there are no departmental spots remaining (i.e. R273, R274, R275 and R276 are all occupied), park the departmental vehicle in surface lot B4.  This is the lot across from Forestry with access off Agronomy Road.
  3. Place a note on the dash indicating the vehicle will be moved to the correct parking spot once the unauthorized user has been removed.  You will find a generic note in the glove compartment that you can place directly on the dash.
  4. When you hand in your keys, notify the receiver of where the vehicle is parked, as there may be another user booked immediately after you.
  5. Notify Lewis Fausak and Andy Jeffries (email to lewis.fausak@ubc.ca and cc andy.jeffries@ubc.ca) of the location and make of the vehicle (i.e. Prius, Caravan, or Ranger). Lewis or Andy will notify UBC Parking of the unauthorized user and will move the departmental vehicle once the unauthorized vehicle has been removed.

[accordion title="Fueling Up"]

To avoid any inconvenience, please fill up the gas tank prior to going out into the fields for research and return the vehicle with at least a half tank of fuel.

Option 1: Fueling Up on Campus 

  1. The campus fuel pump is located in front of the University Services Building.
  2. Record the vehicle mileage, turn off the vehicle, open the fuel cover, and unscrew the cap.
  3. Make sure the pump switch is turned off (flipped down) and use the scanner to scan the RFID chip inside the gas cap (should hear a beep)
  4. Enter the mileage onto the pinpad and press enter (this may take several attempts) and enter the driver code 9999 and press enter. Should read “authorized”.
  5. Flip the pump to on (flipped up), insert the gas nozzle into the vehicle, and begin fueling up.
  6. When you hear a click, return the nozzle to the pump, and switch it off.
  7. See fueling document located in the glovebox for more information.

Option 2: Fuelling Up Off Campus (out of pocket)

  1. Pull up alongside the fueling station with fuel cap on the same side as the pump.
  2. Turn off the vehicle, open the fuel cover, and unscrew the cap.
  3. Use your payment method to select gas for the vehicle.
  4. Insert the nozzle and fuel up until you hear a click.
  5. Return the nozzle and print out your receipt.
  6. If the fuel amount is less that $50, ask Daphne Wang for cash reimbursement and submit your receipt to her. If it is greater than $50, save your receipt and submit your expense claim on workday.
  7. For assistance with workday submission visit: https://ubc.service-now.com/selfservice?id=kb_article&sys_id=c41865b41be2b4900dbaec21b24bcb94&table=kb_knowledge
    • For more details see the fueling document (Expense item: Travel Vehicle and Rental Fuel, Program: PM009555, Cost Center: CC00273, Additional Worktags: FD000)

[/accordion]

[accordion title="Booking Bicycles"]

Due to a limitation in the booking software, you will need to fill out the speed chart field in the form. However, you will not be charged for using the bikes or the trailer.Note: Bell, helmet, and light use (after dark) are MANDATORY for all bikes – failure to use will result in use suspension.

 

Before you go:

  • Check tires for tread wear, debris, and pressure. Add air if needed.
  • Check that the quick release on front wheel is tight and wheel is secure.
  • Squeeze brakes to ensure they are working and strike the rims (not tires), check brake pad condition for wear.
  • Check if the front and back lights are working (if applicable).
  • Check if the bell is working.
  • Inspect your helmet for cracks or signs of wear.

To report any issues with the bike during use/or for assistance with above, contact: Wayne Tamagi wayne.tamagi@ubc.ca (604 822-5694) or Lewis Fausak lewis.fausak@ubc.ca (604-816-5641)

Please ensure that you lock the bicycles and trailer securely to the bike rack. Especially the ones that you are not using as they are locked using the same security cable.
